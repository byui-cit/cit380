Plan B details

Overview

Path B allows for students to still gain experience in becoming more proactive, but with more scaffolding to help them get started. They are given examples and ideas to help scaffold their project planning around. These projects can be completed as a group or individually.

If done individually a Plan B student can get a maximum of 85%. If done as a group the maximum grade is 95%

After settling on a project to complete you should then put some research and thought towards which Project management methodology would best fit with your project plans. Google is your friend here, or here is a sample list to get you started.

Methodology in hand, you should now prepare the Charter for your project and the project management plan. Your documentation list might change slightly based on your methodology, but your deliverable list should include most of the following parts:

  1. Project Charter
  2. Project Management Plan
  3. Scope Management Plan
  4. Schedule Management Plan
  5. Quality Management Plan
  6. Risk Management plan
  7. Communications Plan (if done as a group)
  8. Human Resources Management Plan: (if done as a group)

This list is somewhat open for negotiation. You can omit or add to it provided you support your changes with good rationale.

There will be a requirement for you to turn in a mid-project milestone report detailing your progress at the midpoint of your project, and a lessons learned report you will turn in at the end. The milestone report must be done no later than Week 6, the lessons learned report no later than week 12. Both of these can be completed much earlier if you are trying to finish the course early or are completing multiple projects.

These status reports should be submitted to the Project Milestone Reports discussion board in ILearn. Include any important project documentation or other artifacts that will be needed to understand the full status of your project along with your milestone report.

You get to have some control over the quality management for the project, including how the success of your project will be measured (your grade). The PMBOK has some ideas for this, but here are some additional ideas to look through as you put your plan together:

Additional information

A Project Management Plan (PMP) is a living document. Once you have completed yours make sure to regularly update it as necessary while you execute the project.

There will also be an expectation for you to review other student's projects. You can evaluate the projects either at the half-way point using the milestone mid-project report, or at the end using the lessons learned.

You can determine the format of this review, or you can look at common project post-mortem templates.

Start early on these reviews. Early feedback to the owners of the projects will be much more useful than late feedback. Likewise you should seek out early feedback on your project. Because of the way the course is structured there is no guarantee that your project will get reviewed by a peer. You can seek these out! From classmates, family, co-workers, instructors, peers, etc.

The projects to review will be found in the Project Milestone Reports discussion board in ILearn. Post your review or post-mortem as a reply in that same place.
