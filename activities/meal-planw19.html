Planning a Meal

If you have spent any time cooking for a large group, or preparing anything more complex than soup or sandwiches, you know that there is a lot of preparation that must be done and a lot of details to track for the meal to be a success. Planning and preparing a complex meal really is a very nice example of Project Management.

For this activity you will choose a project management method and then plan a complex meal using it.

  2. Plan a Meal

    Apply your understanding by describing how you would use your selected method to organize, prepare, deliver, and/or clean up a 5 course meal for a large group. Review the following "charter" for more details.

    Dear child,

    You know that you have always been my favorite, and now I'm giving you the chance to prove it! As you are aware it is our turn this year to host the Family Thanksgiving Feast. We haven't had this opportunity for many years and, as you may remember, last time it was a disaster! I might be dead by the time this opportunity comes around again, and your job is to make sure you make this the best Thanksgiving EVER!

    Here are a few details you will need:

    1. We need to locate and secure a great venue to host. Should be able to accommodate up to 40 people. We must leave this venue clean at the end of the event or they will charge us extra...I do not want to pay extra.
    2. The meal must be planned and executed to perfection. The first course ready when the guests arrive, with each additional course prepared and presented in order. No cold mashed potatoes!
    3. You should plan on at least 5 courses. You have some flexibility on what will be served in each of those courses...just don't embarrass me!

    You will have the following responsibilities as the lead on this:

    • Staffing: Make sure you arrange to have enough cooks and servers. These can be recruited from the family. But you should let me know your plan for inviting and coordinating with them.
    • Budget: Make sure to let me know how much the event will cost. You will have at least a venue to rent and food to purchase and prepare.
    • Communications: how will you keep me and everyone else involved up to date on your progress and what they should be aware of?
    • Tracking: how will you keep track of your progress so you know if you are on track to succeed and so that you know what to tell me when I want updates?
    • Risks: what risks will you face? What could derail your plans and cause the dinner to fail? How will you mitigate those risks?
    • Planning: you should have a detailed plan to me no later than then second week in November containing the information above.

    I have the utmost confidence in you, but know that if you fail you will no longer be my favorite and I may never speak to you again!

    Your loving Mother
